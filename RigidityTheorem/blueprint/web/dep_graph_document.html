Definition 1Binary observable

A binary observable on a complex Hilbert space \(H\) is a linear map \(A : H \to H\) that is self-adjoint and satisfies \(A^2 = \mathrm{Id}\).

LaTeX Lean
Definition 3CHSH operator and bias

Given a strategy \(S\), the associated CHSH operator is

\[ \mathrm{CHSH}(A_0,A_1,B_0,B_1) = A_0 \otimes B_0 + A_0 \otimes B_1 + A_1 \otimes B_0 - A_1 \otimes B_1. \]

Its bias on the state \(\psi \) is

\[ \operatorname {Bias}(S) = \Re \langle \psi , \mathrm{CHSH}(A_0,A_1,B_0,B_1)\psi \rangle . \]

Definition 2CHSH strategy

A CHSH strategy consists of:

\[ S = (\psi , A_0, A_1, B_0, B_1), \]

where \(\psi \in H_A \otimes H_B\) is a unit vector and each \(A_i\), \(B_j\) is a binary observable on the corresponding local space.

Definition 9Local extraction isometries

From a CHSH strategy \(S\), the Lean development constructs local maps

\[ V_A : H_A \to Qubit \otimes H_A, \qquad V_B : H_B \to Qubit \otimes H_B, \]

using controlled versions of the observables together with a Hadamard rotation on Alice’s side and a \(\pi /8\)-rotation normalization on Bob’s side.

Lemma 18Bias after extraction

Let

\[ \Psi = \mathrm{regSwap}\bigl((V_A \otimes V_B)\psi \bigr). \]

Then the physical CHSH bias of the strategy is the real part of an inner product between \(\Psi \) and the extracted physical CHSH action.

Theorem 11Exact extracted action of \(A_0\)

For every \(\phi \in H_A \otimes H_B\),

\[ (V_A \otimes V_B)(A_0 \otimes \mathrm{Id})\phi = \bigl((Z \otimes \mathrm{Id})\otimes \mathrm{Id}\bigr)(V_A \otimes V_B)\phi . \]

Equivalently, the norm of the difference is exactly zero.

Theorem 21Alice operator extraction

Assume \(\varepsilon \ge 0\) and \(\operatorname {Bias}(S) \ge 2\sqrt{2} - \varepsilon \). Then there exist isometries \(V_A\), \(V_B\) and a junk state \(\Phi _{\mathrm{junk}}\) such that

\[ \left\lVert \mathrm{regSwap}\bigl((V_A \otimes V_B)(A_0 \otimes \mathrm{Id})\psi \bigr) - (Z \otimes \mathrm{Id})\Phi ^+ \otimes \Phi _{\mathrm{junk}} \right\rVert \le \sqrt{2\sqrt{2}\, \delta (\varepsilon )}, \]

and

\[ \left\lVert \mathrm{regSwap}\bigl((V_A \otimes V_B)(A_1 \otimes \mathrm{Id})\psi \bigr) - (X \otimes \mathrm{Id})\Phi ^+ \otimes \Phi _{\mathrm{junk}} \right\rVert \le \sqrt{c_{\mathrm{CHSH}}\varepsilon } + \sqrt{2\sqrt{2}\, \delta (\varepsilon )}. \]

Theorem 15Approximate anti-commutation

If \(\operatorname {Bias}(S) \ge 2\sqrt{2} - \varepsilon \), then both local anti-commutators are small on the strategy state. More precisely, the Lean development proves

\[ \Re \langle \psi , ((A_1A_0 + A_0A_1)^2 \otimes \mathrm{Id})\psi \rangle \le c_{\mathrm{CHSH}}\varepsilon , \]

and the analogous bound with Alice and Bob swapped.

Theorem 12Exact extracted action of \(B_0\) on the strategy state

Theorem 8Spectral state extraction bound

Suppose \(\Psi \) has unit norm and satisfies

\[ \Re \langle \Psi , (K \otimes \mathrm{Id})\Psi \rangle \ge 2\sqrt{2} - \delta . \]

Then there exists a normalized “junk” vector extracted from the \(\Phi ^+\) Bell component such that

\[ \left\lVert \Psi - \Phi ^+ \otimes \Phi _{\mathrm{junk}} \right\rVert \le \sqrt{2\sqrt{2}\, \delta }. \]

Theorem 20Robust state extraction

Assume \(\operatorname {Bias}(S) \ge 2\sqrt{2} - \varepsilon \) with \(\varepsilon \ge 0\). Then there exist local isometries

\[ V_A : H_A \to Qubit \otimes H_A, \qquad V_B : H_B \to Qubit \otimes H_B, \]

and a junk state \(\Phi _{\mathrm{junk}} \in H_A \otimes H_B\) such that

\[ \left\lVert \mathrm{regSwap}\bigl((V_A \otimes V_B)\psi \bigr) - \Phi ^+ \otimes \Phi _{\mathrm{junk}} \right\rVert \le \sqrt{2\sqrt{2}\, \delta (\varepsilon )}. \]
