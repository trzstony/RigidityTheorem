Let \(K\) be the canonical two-qubit CHSH operator on \(Qubit \otimes Qubit\). In the Bell basis \((\Phi ^+, \Phi ^-, \Psi ^+, \Psi ^-)\), the operator \(K\) is diagonal with eigenvalues

\[ 2\sqrt{2}, \quad 0, \quad 0, \quad -2\sqrt{2}. \]

Theorem 8 Spectral state extraction bound

Uses

  • Proposition 7

Suppose \(\Psi \) has unit norm and satisfies

\[ \Re \langle \Psi , (K \otimes \mathrm{Id})\Psi \rangle \ge 2\sqrt{2} - \delta . \]

Then there exists a normalized “junk” vector extracted from the \(\Phi ^+\) Bell component such that

\[ \left\lVert \Psi - \Phi ^+ \otimes \Phi _{\mathrm{junk}} \right\rVert \le \sqrt{2\sqrt{2}\, \delta }. \]
Proof ▼

The Lean proof expands \(\Psi \) in the Bell basis, uses Proposition 7 to control the weight outside the \(\Phi ^+\) eigenspace, and then normalizes the leading Bell component to define the junk register. This is implemented in ‘StateExtraction.lean‘.
