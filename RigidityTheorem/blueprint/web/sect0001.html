1.1 CHSH Setup

Definition 1 Binary observable
#

Lean declarations

  • Approximate_Rigidity.IsBinaryObservable

A binary observable on a complex Hilbert space \(H\) is a linear map \(A : H \to H\) that is self-adjoint and satisfies \(A^2 = \mathrm{Id}\).

Definition 2 CHSH strategy
#

Lean declarations

  • Approximate_Rigidity.CHSHStrategy

A CHSH strategy consists of:

\[ S = (\psi , A_0, A_1, B_0, B_1), \]

where \(\psi \in H_A \otimes H_B\) is a unit vector and each \(A_i\), \(B_j\) is a binary observable on the corresponding local space.

Definition 3 CHSH operator and bias
#

Uses

  • Definition 2

Lean declarations

  • Approximate_Rigidity.CHSH_op
  • Approximate_Rigidity.chshBias

Given a strategy \(S\), the associated CHSH operator is

\[ \mathrm{CHSH}(A_0,A_1,B_0,B_1) = A_0 \otimes B_0 + A_0 \otimes B_1 + A_1 \otimes B_0 - A_1 \otimes B_1. \]

Its bias on the state \(\psi \) is

\[ \operatorname {Bias}(S) = \Re \langle \psi , \mathrm{CHSH}(A_0,A_1,B_0,B_1)\psi \rangle . \]

Remark 5
#

The final theorems in the Lean development are phrased in terms of the bias lower bound

\[ \operatorname {Bias}(S) \ge 2\sqrt{2} - \varepsilon . \]

This is the normalization that interacts directly with the Bell-space operator \(K\) in the later sections.
