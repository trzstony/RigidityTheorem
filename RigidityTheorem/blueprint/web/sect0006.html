1.6 Bounding trace-norm distance after Alice’s measurement

Lemma 12 Variational form of trace norm (unitary maximum)
#

Lean declarations

  • RigidityToPguess.trace_variational

For any operator \(A\) on a finite-dimensional Hilbert space, one has

\[ \| A\| _1 = \sup _{U\ \mathrm{unitary}} \left|\operatorname {Tr}(UA)\right|. \]
Proof ▼

Step 1 (upper bound). For any unitary \(U\), Hölder duality for Schatten norms gives

\[ \left|\operatorname {Tr}(UA)\right| \le \| U\| _\infty \, \| A\| _1 = \| A\| _1 \]

because \(\| U\| _\infty =1\). Therefore

\[ \sup _{U\ \mathrm{unitary}} \left|\operatorname {Tr}(UA)\right| \le \| A\| _1. \]

Step 2 (lower bound by explicit construction). Let

\[ T:=\sqrt{A^\dagger A}. \]

Choose an orthonormal eigenbasis \((b_i)_i\) of \(T\), with eigenvalues \(\sigma _i\ge 0\) (the singular values of \(A\)). Then

\[ \| A\| _1=\sum _i \sigma _i. \]

Define \(s:=\{ i:\sigma _i\neq 0\} \) and, for \(i\in s\), set

\[ v_i:=\sigma _i^{-1}A b_i. \]

For \(i\notin s\), put \(v_i:=0\). Using

\[ \langle A b_i, A b_j\rangle =\langle b_j,A^\dagger A b_i\rangle =\sigma _i^2\langle b_i,b_j\rangle \]

and \(\| A b_i\| =\sigma _i\), one checks that \((v_i)_{i\in s}\) is orthonormal. Extend it to an orthonormal basis \((c_i)_i\) of the whole space.

Now define the unitary \(U\) by

\[ U(c_i)=b_i\quad \text{for all }i. \]

If \(\sigma _i\neq 0\), then \(A b_i=\sigma _i c_i\), hence

\[ U A b_i=\sigma _i b_i \quad \Rightarrow \quad \langle b_i,U A b_i\rangle =\sigma _i. \]

If \(\sigma _i=0\), then \(A b_i=0\), so the same identity \(\langle b_i,U A b_i\rangle =\sigma _i\) still holds. Therefore, for every \(i\),

\[ \langle b_i,U A b_i\rangle =\sigma _i. \]

Summing over \(i\),

\[ \operatorname {Tr}(UA)=\sum _i \sigma _i=\| A\| _1. \]

Since the right-hand side is nonnegative,

\[ \left|\operatorname {Tr}(UA)\right|=\| A\| _1. \]

Hence

\[ \sup _{U\ \mathrm{unitary}} \left|\operatorname {Tr}(UA)\right|\ge \| A\| _1. \]

Step 3 (conclusion). Combining Steps 1 and 2 yields

\[ \| A\| _1=\sup _{U\ \mathrm{unitary}} \left|\operatorname {Tr}(UA)\right|. \]
Lemma 13 Partial trace is contractive for the trace norm
#

Uses

Lean declarations

  • RigidityToPguess.partial_trace_contractive

For any operator \(H\) on \(R\otimes S\),

\[ \| \operatorname {Tr}_S(H)\| _1 \le \| H\| _1. \]
Proof ▼

By Lemma 12,

\[ \| \operatorname {Tr}_S(H)\| _1 =\sup _{U_R\ \mathrm{unitary}}\left|\operatorname {Tr}\! \left(U_R\, \operatorname {Tr}_S(H)\right)\right| =\sup _{U_R\ \mathrm{unitary}}\left|\operatorname {Tr}\! \left((U_R\otimes I_S)H\right)\right|. \]
\[ \text{Since }U_R\otimes I_S\text{ is unitary on }R\otimes S,\quad \left|\operatorname {Tr}\! \left((U_R\otimes I_S)H\right)\right| \le \sup _{V\ \mathrm{unitary\ on}\ R\otimes S}\left|\operatorname {Tr}(VH)\right| =\| H\| _1. \]

Taking the supremum over \(U_R\) gives \(\| \operatorname {Tr}_S(H)\| _1\le \| H\| _1\).

Lemma 14 Isometries preserve the trace norm
#

Lean declarations

  • RigidityToPguess.isometry_trace_norm

Let \(W\) be an isometry (\(W^\dagger W = I\)). Then \(\| WHW^\dagger \| _1=\| H\| _1\) for all operators \(H\).

Proof ▼

If \(H=U\Sigma V^\dagger \) is a singular value decomposition, then \(WHW^\dagger =(WU)\Sigma (WV)^\dagger \) is also a singular value decomposition because \(WU\) and \(WV\) are isometries. Hence the singular values (and their sum) are unchanged.

Lemma 15 Measurement and discarding systems does not increase trace norm

Let \(\rho _{ABE}\) and \(\sigma _{ABE}\) be two states. Let \(\rho _{XE}\) and \(\sigma _{XE}\) be the associated classical–quantum states obtained by measuring \(A\) in the computational basis and discarding \(B\) as in Definition 4. Then

\[ \| \rho _{XE}-\sigma _{XE}\| _1 \le \| \rho _{ABE}-\sigma _{ABE}\| _1. \]
Proof ▼

Define the measurement isometry \(W:A\to X\otimes A\) by

\[ W := |0\rangle _X\otimes |0\rangle \! \langle 0|_A + |1\rangle _X\otimes |1\rangle \! \langle 1|_A. \]

Let \(\rho ^{\mathrm{meas}}:=(W\otimes I_{BE})\rho _{ABE}(W^\dagger \otimes I_{BE})\) and define \(\sigma ^{\mathrm{meas}}\) similarly. By construction, tracing out \(AB\) produces exactly the states \(\rho _{XE}\) and \(\sigma _{XE}\). Hence

\[ \| \rho _{XE}-\sigma _{XE}\| _1 = \| \operatorname {Tr}_{AB}(\rho ^{\mathrm{meas}}-\sigma ^{\mathrm{meas}})\| _1\le \| \rho ^{\mathrm{meas}}-\sigma ^{\mathrm{meas}}\| _1, \]

where the inequality is Lemma 13 applied to \(H:=\rho ^{\mathrm{meas}}-\sigma ^{\mathrm{meas}}\). Finally, Lemma 14 gives

\[ \| \rho ^{\mathrm{meas}}-\sigma ^{\mathrm{meas}}\| _1 = \| (W\otimes I)(\rho _{ABE}-\sigma _{ABE})(W^\dagger \otimes I)\| _1 = \| \rho _{ABE}-\sigma _{ABE}\| _1. \]
