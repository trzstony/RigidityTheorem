Assume \(\operatorname {Tr}(\rho _{AB}\, \mathrm{CHSH})\ge 2\sqrt2-\varepsilon \). Let \(p:=\langle \Phi ^+|\rho _{AB}|\Phi ^+\rangle \) and define \(\delta :=1-p\). Then

\[ \delta \le \frac{\varepsilon }{2\sqrt2}. \]
Proof ▼

Let \(\mathrm{CHSH}\) have spectral decomposition \( \mathrm{CHSH}=2\sqrt2\, |\Phi ^+\rangle \langle \Phi ^+| -2\sqrt2\, |\Psi ^-\rangle \langle \Psi ^-|. \) (This uses Lemma 7; the two remaining Bell states have eigenvalue \(0\).) Then

\[ \operatorname {Tr}(\rho _{AB}\, \mathrm{CHSH}) = 2\sqrt2\, (p-q)\le 2\sqrt2\, p, \]

where \(q:=\langle \Psi ^-|\rho _{AB}|\Psi ^-\rangle \ge 0\). Rearranging gives \(p\ge 1-\varepsilon /(2\sqrt2)\), i.e. \(\delta \le \varepsilon /(2\sqrt2)\).
